# Who are the peers of Livepeer?

*Clarification.* Some of the figures reported in the survey question were taken from stakingrewards.com. I've since found those figures to be unreliable. The following figures were verified using official sources affiliated to the projects.

* TAO. 6.6%. https://taostats.io/yield

* GRT. https://thegraph.com/explorer/delegate?chain=arbitrum-one

  I'm not very familiar with the revenue model of TheGraph, but based on the information available in the explorer its model appears to be very close to that of Livepeer: GRT holders may delegate their tokens to node operators, which are called *indexers*, in exchange for a cut of their GRT rewards (called *indexing rewards*) and service fees. However, there appear to be significant differences between the indexing rewards earned by different indexers, which would suggest some differences with the Livepeer system.

  Other than Livepeer, the GRT ecosystem also boasts the highest current yield I saw within the DePIN category. Here are some of the key figures:

  * Best APY = 36%. 
  * Best APY with over $1M TVL = 21.2%
  * APY of indexer with largest TVL = 14.32% (20x the TVL of the best APY.) 

* FIL stake pool. 10.3%. https://www.glif.io/en/pool/infinity

  FIL staking and rewards arguably has a similar model to LPT: Filecoin nodes must stake FIL and store data, in exchange for which they receive FIL emissions (block rewards) and fees paid by storage customers. There is no built in delegation system, but third party lending pools such as Glif provide access to FIL yield for investors who don't want to run a FIlecoin mining operation (which is a very capital intensive task). Fees earned from storage customers have historically been negligible compared to FIL emissions, but I haven't looked at for the last year or so so I don't know the latest.

  Filecoin also uses a controller mechanism to target a particular % of staked tokens (which last time I looked was about 30). Unlike LPT, the controller does not operate by adjusting yields; instead, it adjusts the amount of stake the system demands to provide one unit of service (which in the case of Filecoin is 32GiB sealed).

  Unlike LPT, FIL is also used for payments in the Filecoin Protocol. The market stats of Filecoin also differ significantly from Livepeer.
